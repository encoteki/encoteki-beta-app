# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Mixed audience unified by interest in environmental impact through blockchain. Ranges from crypto-native users familiar with wallets and governance to eco-conscious newcomers discovering web3 for the first time. They arrive to mint conservation-linked NFTs, vote on DAO proposals that shape Encoteki's environmental initiatives, and climb a referral leaderboard. Context varies: some are on mobile checking a vote, others are at a desk minting for the first time. The newcomer cohort needs clarity at every step; the crypto-native cohort needs efficiency and trust.

## Product Purpose

Encoteki is a web3 platform where community members mint NFTs, participate in DAO governance, and earn rewards through referrals. NFT ownership grants voting power over proposals that determine real-world environmental and conservation initiatives. The product bridges blockchain mechanics and environmental stewardship, making collective action tangible. Success looks like a growing, active community where minting is effortless, governance participation is high, and newcomers feel welcomed rather than overwhelmed.

## Brand Personality

Grounded, hopeful, communal. The voice is warm and encouraging without being naive. It speaks like a trusted friend who's been doing this work for years: confident but never preachy, optimistic but honest about the challenge. Emotional goals: belonging, quiet confidence, and the feeling that individual action adds up to something real. Inspired by mission-driven brands like Patagonia and Allbirds, where the product and the purpose are inseparable, and the experience feels natural rather than manufactured.

## Anti-references

- **Typical DeFi / crypto dashboards**: No dark-neon-terminal aesthetic, no walls of charts, no jargon-heavy interfaces that assume everyone knows what gas fees are. Encoteki should feel approachable, not like a trading floor.
- **Generic SaaS / corporate**: No hero-metric templates, no sterile white voids, no stock photography. This is a community with a mission, not a B2B conversion funnel.
- **Overly playful / gamified web3**: No pixel art, no cartoon NFT vibes, no meme culture. The environmental mission deserves sincerity and weight.

## Design Principles

1. **Earn trust through clarity**: Every interaction, especially wallet connections and transactions, should feel transparent and understandable. If a newcomer can't tell what's happening, the design has failed.
2. **Warmth over polish**: The interface should feel like it was built by people who care, not by a design system. Natural textures, earthy tones, and human-scale typography over clinical precision.
3. **Collective over individual**: Design should emphasize community participation (governance, leaderboards, shared impact) rather than individual portfolio value. We celebrate what the community does together.
4. **Quiet confidence**: Let the mission speak. No need for loud visuals or aggressive CTAs. The work is serious, the tone is steady, and the design gets out of the way when it should.
5. **Bridge, don't gatekeep**: Every web3 interaction (minting, voting, wallet connection) should be designed for the person who has never done it before, without patronizing those who have.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance: minimum 4.5:1 contrast for normal text, 3:1 for large text. Full keyboard navigation support across all interactive elements. Screen reader compatibility with semantic HTML and ARIA labels where needed. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all animations (already partially implemented). Consider color-blind safe palettes for any data visualization or status indicators in the DAO and leaderboard views.
